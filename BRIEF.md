Project: Learn Tarot — beginner-friendly Tarot learning site

Purpose
- Provide an accessible, trustworthy, and practical learning path for people who want to learn Tarot from first principles through beginner practice to confident reading.
- Combine clear lessons, interactive tools, practical exercises, and a supportive community to turn curiosity into skill.

Primary goals
- Teach foundational Tarot knowledge (cards, suits, archetypes, elemental correspondences).
- Build daily practice routines (card pulls, journaling, spreads).
- Enable users to perform simple readings confidently.
- Offer reusable resources (card meanings library, spread builder, printable worksheets).

Target audience
- Absolute beginners with curiosity about Tarot but no prior background.
- Beginner practitioners who want structured practice and better recall.
- People interested in self-reflection, creative thinking, and symbolic literacy.

User personas
- Persona 1 — Curious Beginner: Age 25–40, enjoys spiritual hobbies, wants guided lessons and simple practice exercises.
- Persona 2 — New Practitioner: Age 30–55, bought a first deck, needs structured study, card drills, and templates for practice.
- Persona 3 — Skeptical Explorer: Age 20–45, curious for psychological/self-help value, wants evidence-based, non-mystical framing.

> Value proposition
>- A gentle, practical, and non-intimidating learning path that turns a beginner into a confident reader through short lessons, hands-on exercises, and tools that reinforce learning.

Core features
- Learning paths: short sequential lessons (Intro → Major Arcana → Minor Arcana → Spreads & Ethics).
- Card library: searchable card pages with upright/reversed meanings, imagery notes, keywords, and journaling prompts.
- Practice tools: daily card draw, randomized card quizzes, timed drills, and a spread builder with step-by-step guidance.
- Guided readings: templates for 1-, 3-, and 10-card readings with interpretation hints for beginners.
- Notebook & journaling: private practice logs, memorization worksheets, and export/print options.
- Community & accountability: optional forum or comments per lesson, weekly group practice prompts.
- Accessibility & inclusivity: plain-language content, translations-ready copy, keyboard navigation, good contrast, and screen-reader support.

Content strategy
- Micro-lessons (5–10 minutes each) that mix theory and immediate practice.
- Progressive release: start with core meanings, then contextual reading practice and spreads.
- Multi-format: short text, illustrated card visuals, audio clips for card meditations, printable PDFs.

Success metrics (KPIs)
- Onboarding completion: % of new signups who complete the first learning path.
- Retention: weekly active users and number of repeat practice sessions per user.
- Engagement: cards saved, journal entries created, spreads built.
- Satisfaction: NPS or simple in-app survey after first month.

Technical & product considerations
- Authentication: optional accounts for journaling / progress (consider social sign-in).
- Content CMS: lightweight headless CMS or Markdown-driven content for lessons and card pages.
- Offline/print: printable PDF lesson packs and simple offline card-page downloads.
- Analytics: basic event tracking for lesson completion and practice tools usage.
- Privacy: journaling and personal data private by default; clear export/delete options.

Accessibility & ethics
 - Use clear, neutral language and optional non-mystical framing for skeptical users.
 - Provide content warnings where content could be sensitive.
 - Ensure WCAG AA contrast and keyboard accessibility.

Styling guidelines
 - Visual tone: clean and minimal with a subtle mystical accent — prioritize legibility and calmness, with a hint of texture or ornamentation used sparingly.
 - Color palette: neutral base (warm off-white, soft gray), high-contrast text (near-black), and one or two accent colors (deep indigo or teal + warm gold) for cards, highlights, and CTAs.
 - Typography: a readable sans-serif for UI and body copy (e.g., Inter or system UI), paired with an elegant serif or display face for headings and card titles to add a gentle mystical feel.
 - Imagery & iconography: use high-quality card imagery with consistent crop and margin; icons should be simple line-work with occasional stylized glyphs for thematic accents.
 - Spacing & layout: roomy white space, 8px-based spacing scale, centered card compositions in reading views, responsive single-column mobile-first layouts.
 - Components: minimal, elevated cards, clear primary and secondary buttons, unobtrusive modals for guided readings, and accessible forms (labels, focus states).
 - Micro-interactions: subtle hover and tap feedback, soft transitions (120–200ms), animated card draws with an option to disable for accessibility.
 - Thematic accents: optional light paper texture or vignette in card preview areas; avoid heavy or distracting backgrounds site-wide.
 - Branding assets: define logo lockups, favicon, and a simple mark for shareable image cards.
 - CSS tokens / variables: establish color, type, radius, and spacing tokens early for consistent theming and easy future customization.
 - Accessibility notes: ensure color-contrast ratios meet WCAG AA, provide reduced-motion preference, and make all interactive elements keyboard-navigable.

Milestones & timeline (example MVP 8–12 weeks)

2. Week 3–4: Lesson pages and basic practice tools (daily draw, journaling), basic auth.
3. Week 5–8: Card library complete, quizzes, spread builder, community basics.
4. Week 9–12: Polish, accessibility testing, analytics, launch checklist.
